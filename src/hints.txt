1- ADHD
Many celebrities, such as Simone Biles, Emma Watson, and Ryan Gosling, have been diagnosed with this condition.
Males are almost three times more likely to be diagnosed with this condition than females.

2- Anxiety
Many celebrities, such as Emma Stone, Adele, and Kristen Stewart, have been diagnosed with this condition.
This condition is among the world’s most common mental disorders, affecting 301 million people in 2019.

3- Autism
This condition does not have a set list of symptoms, with symptoms manifesting in different ways for each person afflicted, making each case unique.
Many individuals with this condition find themselves having difficulties with communication.

4- BPD
Celebrities such as Pete Davidson, Amy Winehouse, and Britney Spears have this condition.
Nearly 75% of people diagnosed with this condition are women, with recent research suggesting that men may be equally affected but are often misdiagnosed.

5- Depression
There is effective treatment for this condition.
Globally, an estimated 280 million people worldwide have this condition, with about 5% of the world’s adults being affected.

6- DID
Many people affected by this disorder have claimed to experience an out-of-body sensation in which they feel detached from their outside surroundings.
About 90%of cases studied in the United States, Canada, and Europe on this condition involve a history of abuse.

7- ED
This condition is very commonly associated with affecting women, with the idea that men may be affected to an equal degree not yet destigmatized.
This condition has the highest mortality rate of any mental illness, and almost 3% of teenagers and an estimated 30 million people in the US are diagnosed with this condition.

8- PTSD
About 6 out of every 100 people in the U.S. will develop this condition at some time during their lives.
This condition affects roughly 13 million people in a given year.
